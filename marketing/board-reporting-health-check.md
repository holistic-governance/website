# Board Reporting Health Check

### A self-assessment for aged care & healthcare boards and executives

Good governance depends on good information. If your board reporting is late, inconsistent, or buried in spreadsheets, decisions get made on gut feel — and gaps in oversight become gaps in compliance.

For Australian aged care and healthcare providers, the stakes are specific: **SIRS** notifications, **care minutes** targets, **mandatory education** completion, **Essential Eight** cyber maturity and the **NSQHS / Aged Care Quality Standards** are all things a board is expected to see clearly and act on. When that information is scattered or manually assembled, three things suffer: **governance** (directors can't discharge their duty of oversight), **regulatory exposure** (issues surface after the threshold, not before), and **decision-making** (no trend, no context, no early warning).

This health check takes about five minutes. Tick each item your organisation genuinely meets today, add up your score, and read the band at the end.

---

## Section 1 — Single source of truth

- [ ] Board reporting draws from **one agreed data source**, not multiple disconnected spreadsheets.
- [ ] Numbers in the board pack **reconcile** with the operational systems they come from (PMS, incident system, HR/LMS).
- [ ] There is **one owner** accountable for the accuracy of the reporting.
- [ ] The same metric is **defined the same way** every month (e.g. how an incident or a "fall" is counted doesn't drift).

## Section 2 — Board-ready presentation

- [ ] Reporting is presented at a **board-appropriate altitude** — summary first, detail on demand, not raw data dumps.
- [ ] Key indicators use **traffic-light / RAG** or clear status indicators a non-clinical director can read in seconds.
- [ ] Metrics are **normalised for comparability** (e.g. incidents per 1,000 occupied bed days, not raw counts).
- [ ] The pack answers "**so what?**" — each metric has a target, threshold, or benchmark to judge it against.

## Section 3 — Regulatory & compliance coverage

- [ ] **SIRS** (Serious Incident Response Scheme) notifications and trends are visible to the board.
- [ ] **Care minutes** delivered vs target (RN and total) are reported and tracked.
- [ ] **Mandatory education / training compliance** rates are reported by cohort or site.
- [ ] **Essential Eight** cyber-security maturity is visible to the board as a governance risk.
- [ ] Performance against **NSQHS Standards** and/or **Aged Care Quality Standards** is mapped and reported.
- [ ] Accreditation status, outcomes and open actions are tracked in the pack.

## Section 4 — Trend & exception visibility

- [ ] Metrics show a **trend over time**, not just a single point-in-time number.
- [ ] The report **flags exceptions past a defined threshold** automatically, so the board's attention is directed.
- [ ] Directors can see **which items are getting worse**, not only current status.
- [ ] Emerging risks are surfaced **before** they become notifiable or reportable events.

## Section 5 — Drill-down to root cause

- [ ] The board summary can be **traced down to the underlying detail** (e.g. from an incident rate to the individual incidents).
- [ ] Quality and risk teams have an **operational view** (incident detail, risk-rating heatmaps, root cause, open disclosure) that feeds the board view.
- [ ] **Root-cause and contributing factors** are captured and reportable, not just incident counts.
- [ ] There is a clear line of sight from a **board-level number to an operational action**.

## Section 6 — Timeliness & automation

- [ ] Board reporting is produced **on a predictable schedule** aligned to the board cycle.
- [ ] Assembling the pack takes **hours, not days** of manual effort.
- [ ] Data is **refreshed automatically or semi-automatically**, not re-keyed each cycle.
- [ ] Ad-hoc questions from directors can be answered **quickly**, without rebuilding a report from scratch.

## Section 7 — Action tracking

- [ ] Every reported issue has a **clear owner and due date**.
- [ ] **Open actions from previous meetings** are visible and their status is tracked to closure.
- [ ] The board can see **whether interventions actually moved the metric**.
- [ ] There is a documented, repeatable **loop** from insight → decision → action → follow-up.

---

## Scoring

Count your ticks (max 30).

| Score | Band | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **0–10** | **At risk** | Reporting is largely manual and fragmented. Governance and regulatory blind spots are likely, and the board is probably reacting to issues rather than seeing them coming. This is the highest-value area to fix first. |
| **11–20** | **Developing** | You have solid foundations but meaningful gaps — often in exception visibility, drill-down, automation or action tracking. A focused build would materially lift board confidence and reduce manual effort. |
| **21–30** | **Board-ready** | Your reporting is mature. The opportunity now is refinement: sharper exception logic, deeper compliance coverage, and reclaiming time through automation. |

Wherever you landed, the pattern is usually the same: the biggest wins come from a **single source of truth**, **automatic exception flagging**, and **clean drill-down** from the board number to the root cause.
